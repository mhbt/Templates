OVERVIEW

Imagine a neighborhood where there’s a guard on every street, watching over your home, your kids as they play in the street, and reporting suspicious activity to police. Sounds like a pretty safe place to live, right? The problem is, live security guards aren’t available everywhere, they get sick, they miss details, and they’re expensive and plain security cameras just confirm something bad already happened. What if you could use modern technology to prevent unwanted activity? Unwanted activity includes theft, speeding vehicles, vandalism, and unknown persons wandering the premises. The Green Zone is a crowd-sourced, smart neighborhood that is monitored and managed 24/7 by AlarmTransfer’s operations center. This is a virtual guard system that elevates the security of entire neighorhoods through persistent surveillance and live intervention. A threat is detected and an operator intervenes, immediately. Finally, you have a chance to stop bad things before they happen!

HOW IT WORKS

The HOA chooses a deployment option - House-to-House or Mesh Network. Cameras are mounted to each home, facing the street, or mounted to lamp posts throughout the neighborhood. Every camera in the neighborhood is connected to hub that allows the enrolled homeowners to access all camera footage, anytime, from anywhere. Advanced video analyics are used to trigger alarm events that live operators respond to by viewing the camera feeds and either speaking directly to the intruder, or reporting suspicious activity directly to local police. Homeowners get access to every camera in the neighborhood for what it would traditionally cost that person to subscribe to cloud video storage for a single camera!

INFRASTRUCTURE REQUIREMENTS

Electrical

120VAC is required at some coordinates.

Lamp Posts

Horizontal viewing angle for all camera pairs is 220° with an max nighttime viewing distance of 100’. Overhead cover is not available.

High-Speed Internet

Internet Service Provider (ISP) will be required to install service connections at the neighborhood entry points.

  • Minimum bandwidth requirement is 75Mbps per camera.
  • Ideal bandwidth requirement is 150Mbps per camera.
  • Available bandwidth will be consumed by data upload, so ask your ISP to match a plan based on upload, not download.

HARDWARE REQUIREMENTS

Special router with load-balancing

These devices will be mounted inside NEMA 3R enclosures at the entrances and be connected to the WAN service connections from the ISP. They will also be installed in each home and are congured to work side-by-side with existing home networking equipment.

Camera System for Entrances

This unit provides 4MP HD image quality and a variable lens. When combined with the analytics of Milestone’s software, we will capture and log license plates and identify people as they leave the neighborhood day and night.

Special access point at each camera site

This device will be mounted to each lamp post alongside the camera. Each of these serve as a “node” on the neighborhood’s mesh network. Each node must connect to at least two other nodes.

Camera System for Homes

Camera System for Lamp Posts

HOUSE-TO-HOUSE EXAMPLE DEPLOYMENT

Plan Phase

On contract signing, AlarmTransfer will engage governing members of the HOA to build a communication plan to homeowners. Camera installation at the entrances must be scheduled 2 weeks in advance and depends on power and internet service installation. Duration estimate for physical camera installation at the entrances is 2 days. Camera configuration and testing will be estimated in the quote. AlarmTransfer will provide three (3) camera options for homeowners and present the options to the HOA. There will be a minimum quality standard that must be maintained to ensure integrity of the video and monitoring service. However, some homeowners may opt to pay a premium for a “better” camera. AlarmTransfer will help craft the message to homeowners and provide a registration page on our website to enroll homeowners. The HOA will be responsible for directing the homeowners to the registration page and driving adoption by the community. This helps eliminate unwanted solicitation from negatively impacting the community. Details will be FInalized during this phase of the project.

Execute Phase

This phase begins once power and internet are available and installation is scheduled at least 2 weeks in advance. The cameras at each main entrance will be mounted within 2 days of project start. The HOA distributes information about how to enroll to the homeowners. As homeowners visit the online registration page, they schedule the date and time of their camera installation. AlarmTransfer technicians travel to each home in the neighborhood, installing camera systems and adding each camera to the system. An access list is maintained by AlarmTransfer and synchronized with the HOA on a scheduled basis.

Monitoring

Once a camera is enrolled, it becomes part of a monitored system of sensors. AlarmTransfer’s operations center is conducting virtual guard tours and responding to event-based alerts triggered by The Green Zone, 24/7/365. This is a form of persistent surveillance that is used in fusion cells and command centers in many major cities with great success. AlarmTransfer consolidates the scope into single neighborhoods. Over time, patterns of life are “learned” into the video analytics platform and the system becomes more intelligent. Each camera is watching the street and the house across the street with all windows masked for privacy. The AlarmTransfer operations center continuously configures the video analytics platform to “learn” normal activity for the neighborhood. Other analytics can be enabled, such as vehicle speed detection. This activity triggers alerts inside the operations center and helps AlarmTransfer report real-time intelligence to local law enforcement. Threat alerts can be automatically e-mailed or transmitted via SMS to homeowners. For example, when a suspicious person is spotted during a virtual guard tour, the AlarmTransfer operations center can release a snapshot of the person with a brief description of activity to all homeowners. Parents with children playing outside have an extra set of eyes with The Green ZoneTM.

Maintenance/Support Phase

Technical support and troubleshooting will be provided directly to the homeowner by AlarmTransfer for the life of the service contract. For the House-to-House option, each homeowner must sign an individual monitoring agreement that authorizes “public” access to the camera feed of the enrolled camera and authorizes AlarmTransfer to access the camera via secure, remote connection in order to perform camera maintenance, firmware upgrades, reconfiguration, remote reboot, etc…

Unlike typical commercial and residential monitored camera deployments, there is no hourly rate assessed to troubleshoot problematic cameras enrolled in The Green ZoneTM program. AlarmTransfer is responsible for routine care and maintenance of the cameras and ensuring they’re functioning properly in the overall system. AlarmTransfer is responsible for maintaining uptime of the mesh network and will do so at no additional cost to the HOA. Replacement hardware that is required do to end-of-life or damages from vandalism, theft, or natural disaster will be billed to the HOA. Leasing options are available to combine the cost of hardware, software, and services and spread the total cost over a term, such as 5 years. After the lease term, the monthly obligation to the HOA would be reduced to only monitoring fees.

How Effective is The Green Zone

"A pilot program for public surveillance was initiated in Baltimore in 2005 to observe the impact of modern security systems on rising crime rates. The neighborhoods involved in the program were known for having a high level of criminal activity. Four months after the cameras were put into place, the crime rate decreased by approximately 30 incidents per month. The number held steady for the following three years which clearly shows that the installation of surveillance cameras played a major hand in lowering criminal activity in crime prone neighborhoods of Baltimore. According to researchers, the estimated benefits of averting the crimes in Baltimore (savings in criminal justice costs) outweighed the costs of setting up and operating the surveillance system. "

The Green Zone is the most advanced, well optimized and comprehensive neighborhood surveillance system available in the country today. If the pilot program could help bring down crime rates in Baltimore, then one can only wonder how effective The Green Zone will be in keeping neighborhoods safe and secure.
